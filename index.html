A Quick Synopsis

  • 2015-2017

    UCSD

    When I started college, I was an undeclared major because none of my high school courses resonated within me. Inspired by my passion for creating products that others can enjoy or solutions that make other people's lives more convenient, I decided to take my first computer science class where I quickly fell in love with programming. At the time, changing majors to Computer Science was based off of a lottery system, so I gladly settled with changing my major to Interdisciplinary Computing and the Arts (ICAM).

  • 2017-2019

    SCU

    Eventually, I craved more algorithmic and theoretical depth than what my ICAM courses provided, and took the initiative to transfer to SCU so that I could finally fulfill my desire of becoming a CS major. I was grateful to be able to take CS courses while working two jobs and competitively boxing.

  • 2019

    App Academy

    Right after graduating from SCU with my degree in CS, I began a coding bootcamp to sharpen my hands on experience. The experience I obtained from creating full stack projects at App Academy combined with the math, theory, and algorithms from my university courses quickly made me into a thorough, well-rounded software engineer.

  • 2020

    Full Stack Web Developer

    Under Tutoria, I contributed to React and json legacy code. I was always eager to help improve the features for Tutoria's hundreds of clients and volunteers while working with a team of international developers.

  • 2020

    WaterGuru

    At WaterGuru, I queried, refined, and analyzed data from multiple sources including AWS Aurora, AWS Dynamo, Woo Commerce, SalesForce, and spreadsheets via various Python libraries in order to create interactive visualizations and influential meetings for the company heads, software engineers, and test engineers resulting in constant improvement of product performance and customer satisfaction. I created and managed a Dash Plotly SPA connected to a Postgresql database that visualizes WaterGuru's data, and updates its data visualizations on a daily basis.

  • 2022

    Technical Support Engineer

    At Accenture, I've refined cloud platform and API support skills by assisting developers with platform level troubleshooting, code debugging, and application service onboarding via the utilization of technical reproductions, first level bug triaging and elevations (through SFDC and issue tracker), and production and maintenance of standardized technical documentation.
